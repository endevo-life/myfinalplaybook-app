# Q12 Mobile Product Plan

## Product decision
The mobile app should not give users a long PDF as the main experience. On a phone, the better product is a daily action app.

## What happens when a user opens the app
1. They see a short intro and start the 12-question assessment.
2. They answer one question at a time.
3. They see their score and weakest domain.
4. They get Day 1 immediately.
5. They return daily for the next action.

## Why PDF is not the best phone experience
- PDFs are harder to read on small screens.
- PDFs are easy to ignore after download.
- PDFs do not naturally support tracking.
- PDFs do not encourage daily return behavior.

## Better mobile experience
- One action card per day.
- A clear completion button.
- A progress indicator like 2 of 7 done.
- Short support copy instead of long pages.
- Push reminders linked directly to the day’s action.

## Engagement model
- Day 1: instant win.
- Days 2 to 6: one action per day.
- Day 7: summary and next-step CTA.
- Reminder notifications if the user has not returned.

## How to keep users engaged
- Show progress visually.
- Celebrate each completed day.
- Keep actions short and specific.
- Make returning easy with deep links.
- Use reminders instead of expecting self-motivation.

## Tracking strategy
Track these events in the app and send them to your CRM or analytics tool:
- Assessment started.
- Assessment completed.
- Email captured.
- Result viewed.
- Day 1 opened.
- Day 1 completed.
- Day 2 opened.
- Day 2 completed.
- Reminder opened.
- PDF exported.
- Book call clicked.

## What to do with the existing GHL setup
- Keep GHL for contact creation and workflow automation.
- Trigger tags based on score band or weakest domain.
- Trigger reminder sequences based on app events.
- Trigger follow-up messages if the user stops engaging.

## Re-engaging the previous 197 users
- Rebrand the app as a simpler mobile experience.
- Tell them they can now get one action a day on their phone.
- Give them an easy resume path.
- Use email plus push reminders.
- Reopen the loop with a quick first win, not a long re-onboarding.

## Free or low-cost version
The app can stay cost-friendly if you:
- Use Expo.
- Reuse the existing quiz logic.
- Keep the first release focused.
- Use optional PDF export only.
- Avoid expensive custom infrastructure until the app proves engagement.
